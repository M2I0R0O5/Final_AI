Official GPA Rule — Ilia State University (translated)

1. At Ilia State University, GPA (Grade Point Average) is the sum of the products of each course's GP (Grade Point) and its credits, divided by the total credits of the courses the student has taken:

GPA = Σ (GP of each course × credits of that course)  /  Σ (credits of each course)

2. GP (Grade Point) is the score received for a course, converted onto a 4-point scale.

3. Under the GP system, grades fall into the following categories:

Score-to-letter categories

0–5051–6061–7071–8081–9091–9596–100

4. Within the GP system, a grade of E (51–60) is always fixed at 1.00 GP. Grades of D, C, B and A (61 through 95 points) increase uniformly, in steps of 1/12 (≈0.08), as the score rises. A grade of A+ (96–100 points) is fixed at 4.00 GP.

5. GPA is issued for all academic programmes at Ilia State University except the doctoral level. GPA is also not issued for certificate programmes.

6. Ilia State University issues only the final GPA, which appears in the diploma supplement. This does not apply to the one-cycle programme in Medicine.

7. When the final GPA is calculated, the result is rounded to one decimal place.

Footnote: the fixed ("frozen") GP within the E and A+ bands reflects the idea that differences inside those bands are not meaningful — any score from 51–60 is treated as equally weak, and any score from 96–100 is treated as equally outstanding.
